How this page should be used

After sitting up straight and clicking on "Start video" you should click on "Capture current position" to save the current calculated distance and vertical position of your head, and use that as a baseline. Upon clicking the page will try to measure your distance from the webcam ever 1500 milliseconds, and compare it to the captured distance. By default, if the distance difference is greater than 5% you will be notified every 15 seconds, also by default. You can control these values on the page.

You might need to stop the video feed to allow other apps to use the camera. You can also hide the video output to reduce resource usage by the page.

Info & disclaimer

This page uses simple math to calculate your approximate distance to the camera using data from a client side facial detection library. This gives it a value to compare against and check if you are getting closer to your camera, or lowering your head, which would indicate that you might be slouching. It then sends you a local notification for you to pull back and straighten up.

In no way does the page send any client data back to any servers. This is purely a client side implementation.
